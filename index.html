Whatcom Deserves a Public Hospital

We are worth it. Whatcom County residents deserve a hospital or a network of clinics that works more closely in tune with the people. It won't solve every problem immediately yet it is worth doing. It may be a long term project but we can build momentum.

Rome wasn't built in a day. If you plan for Whatcom's future, plan for a public hospital.

Any implementation of a public hospital or network of clinics must be wide open for discussion and analysis. It would be a subject of democratic processes. It would be ours.

.
We don't have a public hospital?

Not in Whatcom County. We have PeaceHealth which is described as a non-profit with religious affiliations that receives tax breaks to operate.

Do other counties that are partly or significantly rural have public hospitals?

There are less rural serving public hospitals. But this is not a reason to not have one... it may be more of a reason to have one. More research to come.

Better Off Public: Quality of Care

PeaceHealth comes at a cost. PeaceHealth has made cuts to services and made a push for providers to see more patients1. Additionally, PeaceHealth billed thousands of low-income patients without notifying them that they were likely eligible for financial assistance with their hospital bills2. A public hospital could be more responsive and funded with increased tax dollars as PeaceHealth currently benefits unfairly from reduced taxes.

Better Off Public: Freedom of Religion in Healthcare

Promote freedom of expression and protections for more diversity within healthcare by providing a public hospital. This includes for those of different faiths and secular people alike than just currently promoted by PeaceHealth.
